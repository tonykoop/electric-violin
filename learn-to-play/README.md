# Electric Violin — Learn to Play

Starter songbook for Tony Koop's electric violin build.

## Tuning

Standard violin tuning — **GDAE** (low to high), identical to acoustic violin.
Plug into your amp or audio interface before tuning; the piezo/magnetic pickup
can make open-string tuning harder to hear unplugged.

| String | Pitch |
|--------|-------|
| 4 (lowest) | G3 |
| 3 | D4 |
| 2 | A4 |
| 1 (highest) | E5 |

## Tone tips for electric

- Start with a clean amp channel and no effects while learning tunes.
- Gain/volume: set low enough that you can hear your bow hair on the string,
  not just the amp.
- EQ: boost low-mids slightly (around 400 Hz) to approximate the body resonance
  that an acoustic body provides.

## First-position range

Same as acoustic violin: G3–A5 in first position. All tunes here stay in
first position.

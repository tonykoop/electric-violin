# Go Tell Aunt Rhody

**Provenance:** Traditional American folk song, derived from an 18th-century
melody attributed to Rousseau. Public domain.

**Range:** D4–C5. All notes in first position on G and D strings.

**Practice tips:**
- This tune has only 8 distinct pitches — it's about tone quality, not
  complexity.
- Play the first bar four times with different bow strokes: (1) full bow,
  (2) upper half only, (3) lower half only, (4) spiccato (bouncing). Notice
  how the electric pickup captures each difference.
- The repeated-note figure (E E E E in bar 3) is a great test of consistent
  bow pressure — each note should sound identical in volume and tone.

# Cripple Creek

**Provenance:** Traditional Appalachian fiddle tune and banjo tune. Public domain.

**Range:** E4–E5. A major key — bright and open on violin.

**Fiddle style for electric violin:**
- Keep the bow short and near the tip for that dry fiddle chop.
- The electric violin can produce a lovely clean twang on A strings — use it.
- At ♩=116 the tune has real momentum; don't rush the A section's long final
  note (A8 = 4 beats of A). Let it ring and then cut it clean.

**Practice tips:**
- The B section has a three-bar descending sequence (e-e-d, e-e-d, e-d-c) —
  once you feel the pattern it almost plays itself.
- Open A string (bar 1, beat 1) is the anchor. Come back to it confidently
  each time.

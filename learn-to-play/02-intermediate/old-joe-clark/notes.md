# Old Joe Clark

**Provenance:** Traditional American fiddle tune, widely collected in the
southern Appalachians. Public domain.

**Range:** D4–E5. A Dorian (A B C D E F G A) — natural minor with a raised
sixth. The F♯ doesn't appear, so no accidentals needed.

**Why intermediate:**
- ♩=120 is a brisk dance tempo. Clean string crossings at this speed require
  efficient bow arm.
- The B section stays higher on the fingerboard (C5–E5 range).

**Electric violin note:**
- This tune benefits from a slight reverb effect once you're comfortable.
  A small room reverb gives it a live-dance-hall feel without muddying the
  articulation.

**Practice tips:**
- Learn at ♩=80, then bump up 8 BPM at a time once each section is clean.
- The B section cross-string pattern (c–d–e–d) on A and E strings: practice
  slowly as a string-crossing drill before adding full tempo.
